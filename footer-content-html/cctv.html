IP-CCTV Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16.04.2024

Premier Restaurants Romania S.R.L ("the Company" or "we"), located in Bucharest, America House Building, Nicolae Titulescu Blvd no. 4-8, West Wing, 5th Floor, Sector 1, is the data controller of your personal data processed through the IP-CCTV closed-circuit television system.

The Company may update or modify this policy, in which case we will inform you by publishing the new version on the Company's website, https://www.mcdonalds.ro ("the Site"). The updated (or modified) policy will take effect and begin to be enforceable from the date it is published on the Site.

If you have any questions regarding this IP-CCTV Privacy Policy, you can contact us by email at gdpr@ro.mcd.com or by mail or courier at the address in Bucharest, Nicolae Titulescu Blvd no. 4-8, America House Building, West Wing, 5th Floor, Sector 1.

General Information about the IP-CCTV System

The Company operates a closed-circuit television (CCTV) system – IP-CCTV, exclusively to ensure the safety and security of people and property, to prevent, discourage, and manage security incidents, and to prevent criminal activities. This system is part of the measures supporting the Company's security policies.

The IP-CCTV system is a conventional system. All surveillance cameras operate 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

The IP-CCTV system monitors the minimum necessary areas to ensure the safety and security of people and property at the Company's locations.

There are no surveillance cameras in areas that provide a higher degree of privacy (such as, for example, employee changing rooms or restrooms).

Personal Data Processed by the IP-CCTV System

If you visit the Company's headquarters or our restaurants and cafes (inside buildings such as shopping malls or on terraces), we may process your image captured by the IP-CCTV system.

Purposes and Legal Grounds of Processing

We process your image captured by the IP-CCTV system exclusively for the purpose of ensuring safety and security, guarding and protecting people and property. We process this data based on our legitimate interest (Art. 6 (1) (f) GDPR) or to comply with legal provisions regarding the protection of premises, goods, valuables, and personal protection (Art. 6 (1) (b) GDPR).

Data Retention

We retain the images captured by the IP-CCTV system for a minimum of 20 days, and a maximum of 30 days from the time they were captured. However, if the images are used in an investigation of incidents, we may keep them until the investigation is completed.

Transfer of Data Captured by the IP-CCTV System

The Company is committed to protecting your image captured by the IP-CCTV system. For the purposes of data processing, the Company may transfer such data to the following categories of recipients:

  • Contractual partners of the Company, specializing in providing services related to the operation, technical support, and maintenance of the IP-CCTV system. The Company carefully selects these providers and ensures they only process your personal data according to the Company's instructions and only for the purpose of providing the contracted services;
  • Other companies within the group of which the Company is a part;
  • Public authorities, in response to their requests for information or to protect the rights, assets, or safety of the Company;
  • For the protection of our rights in legal disputes;
  • In connection with or during negotiations related to the restructuring of the Company, financing, or transactions involving the Company or part of it, including, for example, transactions involving the transfer of the Company or part of it to another entity.

No routine or periodic transfers occur.

Your Rights as a Data Subject

As a data subject, you have the following rights, according to the applicable legal provisions:

  • The right to be informed about the processing of your personal data;
  • The right to access your data, i.e., to request confirmation from the Company about whether or not your data is being processed, and, if so, to access the data and details regarding the processing activities;
  • The right to rectification, i.e., the right to have inaccurate data corrected and incomplete data completed without undue delay;
  • The right to erasure ("the right to be forgotten"), i.e., the right to obtain the erasure of your personal data without undue delay, provided that the conditions of applicable legislation are met;
  • The right to restrict processing under certain conditions set by applicable law;
  • The right to data portability, i.e., (i) the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and (ii) the right to transmit these data to another data controller without hindrance from the Company, provided that the conditions set by law are met;
  • The right to object, i.e., the right to object to the processing of your data based on the Company's legitimate interest, including profiling, unless the Company has legitimate and compelling reasons for processing that override your interests, rights, and freedoms, or the processing is necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defense of a legal claim;
  • The right not to be subject to automated decision-making, i.e., the right to obtain human intervention regarding such decisions;
  • The right to withdraw consent when your personal data is processed based on your consent;
  • The right to file a complaint with the National Supervisory Authority for Personal Data Processing or with the competent courts, as necessary.

You can exercise your rights at any time by sending a request to the address in Bucharest, Nicolae Titulescu Blvd no. 4-8, America House Building, West Wing, 5th Floor, Sector 1, or by email at gdpr@ro.mcd.com.

Data Protection Officer

The processing of personal data is monitored and supervised by the Data Protection Officer, who can be contacted at gdpr@ro.mcd.com.

This IP-CCTV Privacy Policy complements the Company's Privacy Policy, which is posted on the Company's website at https://www.mcdonalds.ro.
